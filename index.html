üìñ Terminologies Glossary

üí° Quick Reference: Essential terms for VA Trust Accounting. Use search to find specific definitions quickly.
üè¶ Trust Accounting Terms
Trust Account
Separate bank account holding client funds (rental bonds, deposits, rent) that must be kept distinct from agency operating funds. Highly regulated by state/territory legislation.
Rental Trust
Trust account specifically for rental property funds including rent receipts, bonds, and landlord disbursements. Must maintain positive balance for each property.
Sales Trust
Trust account holding deposits and funds related to property sales transactions until settlement completion.
Statutory Deposit
Minimum amount required by law to be held in trust account as financial security for client funds, typically $2,000-$10,000 depending on jurisdiction.
Trust Ledger
Detailed record of all trust account transactions for each property or client, showing receipts, disbursements, and running balance.
Reconciliation
Process of matching bank statement transactions to accounting system entries to ensure accuracy and identify discrepancies. Required daily/monthly for trust accounts.
Fiduciary Duty
Legal obligation to act in best interests of clients when handling their funds, maintaining highest standards of care and honesty.
Controlled Monies
Funds held by agent on behalf of clients that must be accounted for separately (e.g., rental income, security deposits).
üèòÔ∏è Property Management Terms
Lessor / Landlord
Property owner who leases their property to tenants through property management agency.
Lessee / Tenant
Person or entity renting a property from the landlord under a lease agreement.
Bond / Security Deposit
Refundable amount held as security against property damage or unpaid rent, typically 4 weeks' rent. Must be lodged with government authority in most states.
Arrears
Overdue rent payments owed by tenant. Tracked separately and may trigger legal action if not resolved.
Outgoings
Expenses paid from rental income such as strata fees, council rates, water charges, or maintenance costs as per management agreement.
Disbursement
Payment made from trust account to landlord (rental income) or vendor (sale proceeds) after deducting fees and expenses.
Management Fee
Commission charged by property manager, typically 5-10% of rental income, deducted before disbursement to landlord.
Settlement
Completion of property sale where ownership transfers and funds are distributed to vendor (minus commissions and costs).
Vendor
Property seller in a sales transaction.
Purchaser
Property buyer in a sales transaction who pays deposit into sales trust account.
üí∞ Financial & Accounting Terms
General Ledger (GL)
Complete record of all financial transactions for the business, organized by account type (assets, liabilities, income, expenses).
Debit (DR)
Entry on left side of ledger showing increase in assets or expenses, or decrease in liabilities or income.
Credit (CR)
Entry on right side of ledger showing decrease in assets or expenses, or increase in liabilities or income.
Journal Entry
Manual accounting entry to record transaction in system, showing debits and credits that must balance.
Accounts Receivable (AR)
Money owed to agency by clients (unpaid management fees, invoices). Tracked until payment received.
Accounts Payable (AP)
Money agency owes to suppliers or contractors for services/goods received but not yet paid.
Variance
Difference between expected and actual amounts in reconciliation or budget analysis requiring investigation.
Accrual
Recording revenue or expenses when earned/incurred, not when cash is received/paid. Important for accurate financial reporting.
Trial Balance
Report showing all ledger account balances at specific date. Total debits must equal total credits.
Chart of Accounts
Organized list of all accounts in accounting system, typically numbered and categorized by type (assets, liabilities, equity, income, expenses).
GST (Goods & Services Tax)
10% tax on most goods and services in Australia. Property management fees are GST-inclusive; residential rent is GST-free.
BAS (Business Activity Statement)
Quarterly tax form reporting GST collected and paid, PAYG withholding, and other tax obligations to ATO.
üìä Compliance & Audit Terms
Audit Trail
Complete chronological record of transactions with supporting documentation, allowing auditors to trace any entry back to source.
Statutory Audit
Legally required audit of trust accounts (typically annual) by external auditor to verify compliance with regulations.
Compliance Certificate
Document issued by auditor confirming trust accounts meet legislative requirements and are properly maintained.
Internal Controls
Policies and procedures to prevent fraud, ensure accuracy, and maintain regulatory compliance (e.g., dual authorization, segregation of duties).
Breach
Violation of trust account regulations (e.g., negative ledger balance, unauthorized withdrawal). Must be reported to regulatory authority immediately.
Regulatory Authority
Government body overseeing property industry (e.g., NSW Fair Trading, Consumer Affairs Victoria). Enforces trust account laws and investigates complaints.
AASB (Australian Accounting Standards Board)
Body setting accounting standards in Australia that govern financial reporting and disclosure requirements.
üíª System & Software Terms
PropertyMe
Cloud-based property management software for residential rentals, including trust accounting, tenant management, and reporting features.
Console Cloud
Comprehensive property management platform with trust accounting, CRM, and compliance tools for real estate agencies.
MYOB (Mind Your Own Business)
Popular Australian accounting software for general ledger, invoicing, payroll, and financial reporting.
Xero
Cloud accounting software with bank feeds, invoicing, expense tracking, and real-time financial reporting capabilities.
Bank Feed
Automated import of bank transactions into accounting software, reducing manual data entry and improving reconciliation speed.
Batch Processing
Processing multiple transactions simultaneously (e.g., monthly disbursements to all landlords) rather than individually for efficiency.
üìã Document & Report Terms
Trust Account Statement
Monthly statement showing property owner their rental income, deductions, and balance held in trust. Required by law in most states.
Reconciliation Report
Document showing bank balance matches accounting system balance, listing outstanding transactions and discrepancies.
Aged Receivables
Report categorizing unpaid invoices by age (0-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+ days) to identify collection priorities.
Financial Statements
Formal reports on financial position including Balance Sheet, Profit & Loss Statement, and Cash Flow Statement.
Supporting Documentation
Source documents (invoices, receipts, bank statements, contracts) that prove transaction legitimacy and must be retained for audit.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Beginner Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

‚ùå Mistake #1: Using Wrong Property Code
Impact: Rent allocated to wrong landlord, requires reversal
How to Avoid: ALWAYS double-check property code before saving. Verify tenant name matches property. Use search function to confirm.
‚ùå Mistake #2: Processing Without Authorization
Impact: Unauthorized payment, potential compliance breach
How to Avoid: Never process payments >$5,000 without dual approval. Check authorization matrix. When in doubt, ask first.
‚ùå Mistake #3: Skipping Daily Reconciliation
Impact: Errors compound, discrepancies harder to find later
How to Avoid: Reconcile EVERY day without exception. Make it first priority before processing transactions. Set calendar reminder.
‚ùå Mistake #4: Poor Documentation
Impact: Failed audits, can't trace transactions, compliance issues
How to Avoid: Attach documentation to EVERY transaction. Use clear file names. Write detailed notes for unusual items.
‚ùå Mistake #5: Not Asking for Help
Impact: Mistakes snowball, stress increases, quality suffers
How to Avoid: Ask questions immediately when unsure. Better to ask 100 questions than make 1 error. No question is "stupid."
‚ùå Mistake #6: Ignoring Discrepancies
Impact: Small errors become big problems, compliance breaches
How to Avoid: Investigate ALL discrepancies same day. Never assume "it'll work itself out." Document investigation even if resolved.

üö© Red Flags Auditors Look For

üö® Red Flag #1: Negative Ledger Balances
What auditors check: Individual property ledgers showing negative balances at any time
Why it's serious: Indicates trust fund was used for wrong property (compliance breach)
How to prevent: Daily balance verification, automated alerts for negatives
If found: Document date, amount, cause, correction, and prevention measures
üö® Red Flag #2: Missing Documentation
What auditors check: Random transactions without invoices, receipts, or authorization
Why it's serious: Cannot verify legitimacy of transaction
How to prevent: Mandatory documentation attachment policy, no exceptions
If found: Retrieve documentation immediately, explain reason for missing docs
üö® Red Flag #3: Reconciliation Gaps
What auditors check: Missing monthly reconciliations or delayed reconciliations
Why it's serious: Regulatory requirement to reconcile monthly (max 5 business days)
How to prevent: Calendar reminders, never skip reconciliation
If found: Complete missing reconciliations ASAP, document reason for delay
üö® Red Flag #4: Trust/Operating Fund Mixing
What auditors check: Operating expenses paid from trust, or trust deposits to operating account
Why it's serious: Serious breach, can result in license suspension
How to prevent: Absolute separation, different bank accounts, different signatories
If found: Immediately notify compliance officer, report to regulator if required
üö® Red Flag #5: Unauthorized Payments
What auditors check: Large payments (>$5,000) without dual authorization
Why it's serious: Fraud risk, internal control weakness
How to prevent: Strict authorization matrix, electronic approval workflow
If found: Verify payment was legitimate, strengthen authorization procedures
üö® Red Flag #6: Stale Outstanding Items
What auditors check: Reconciling items >90 days old not investigated
Why it's serious: Indicates errors not being resolved
How to prevent: Monthly review of outstanding items, timely follow-up
If found: Research immediately, clear or explain each item
